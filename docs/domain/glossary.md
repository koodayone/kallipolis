# Glossary

This glossary defines the domain vocabulary that appears throughout the Kallipolis documentation. Most entries are California-specific institutional names, classification systems, or policy concepts. Where a term has full treatment in another document, the entry includes a link.

---

**AB 1602.** California Assembly Bill 1602, signed in 2016, which created the Strong Workforce Program. Codified as California Education Code Part 54.5 (sections 88820–88833). See [Strong Workforce Program](./strong-workforce-program.md).

**California Community Colleges (CCC).** The largest community college system in the United States, serving 2.2 million students across 116 colleges. Governed at the state level by the Chancellor's Office in Sacramento. See [California Community College System](./california-community-college-system.md).

**Career Technical Education (CTE).** The umbrella term for educational programs focused on preparing students for careers in specific industries. CTE includes both credit and noncredit programs and is the primary site of workforce development activity in the California community college system.

**Centers of Excellence for Labor Market Research (COE).** A network of regional centers across California that produces sector profiles, career pathway analyses, employment trends, wage data, and job growth projections for the California Community Colleges. The leading source of labor market research for the system. See [Data Authorities](./data-authorities.md).

**Chancellor's Office.** The state-level administrative body for the California Community Colleges, located in Sacramento. Sets strategic direction in coordination with the Board of Governors, administers programs including the Strong Workforce Program, and operates system-wide infrastructure including the MIS Data Mart and the NOVA planning system.

**Classification of Instructional Programs (CIP).** A federal classification system that organizes educational programs by content area. Used for federal reporting on degree and certificate offerings and adopted by California institutions where federal coordination is required.

**Data Mart (DataMart).** Common shorthand for the California Community Colleges Chancellor's Office Management Information System (MIS) Data Mart, the system of record for student-level data across the entire community college system. See [Data Authorities](./data-authorities.md).

**Employment Development Department (EDD).** The California state agency responsible for unemployment insurance, disability insurance, paid family leave, payroll tax administration, and labor market information. EDD is the authority for employer data because its administrative role gives it comprehensive coverage of every California employer. The agency's Labor Market Information Division (LMID) publishes the analytical product of this administrative reach. See [Data Authorities](./data-authorities.md).

**Middle skill credential.** A certificate, associate's degree, or industry-recognized credential that is less than a bachelor's degree but more than a high school diploma. The legislative findings that established the Strong Workforce Program identify middle skill credentials as the primary gateway to careers in California's economy.

**NOVA.** The Chancellor's Office online platform through which Strong Workforce Program applications are submitted and SWP-funded work is reported. Where consortia submit their plans, where districts submit project applications, and where outcome data is tracked across the program's reporting cycles. See [Strong Workforce Program](./strong-workforce-program.md).

**Regional consortium.** An administrative grouping of community college districts organized for regional coordination and joint planning of workforce development activity. Each district participates in exactly one consortium, and consortia administer the regional share of Strong Workforce Program funding for their region.

**Standard Occupational Classification (SOC).** A federal classification system that organizes jobs into occupational categories. Used by the Bureau of Labor Statistics and adopted by California institutions for labor market analysis. SOC codes are how the same occupation can be tracked across regions, industries, and employers in a comparable way.

**Strong Workforce Program (SWP).** California's recurring annual investment in career technical education at the community college and K–12 levels, established in 2016 by AB 1602. Funded at $290 million per year and framed by the Chancellor's Office around two complementary objectives: *more* (more students in high-demand programs) and *better* (better completion, employment, and earnings outcomes). See [Strong Workforce Program](./strong-workforce-program.md).

**Taxonomy of Programs (TOP).** A California classification system that organizes community college programs and the courses within them by subject area. Used by the Chancellor's Office for system-wide reporting and by Kallipolis for relating courses to occupations and to student enrollment distributions.

**Vision 2030.** The California Community Colleges system's strategic framework, refined in 2025, centered on equity in access, success, and support. Treats workforce mobility and community partnerships with labor and industry as strategic pillars. See [California Community College System](./california-community-college-system.md).

**Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act (WIOA).** Federal workforce legislation (Public Law 113-128) that establishes the framework for state and regional workforce development planning, performance accountability, and labor market analysis. The Strong Workforce Program is required by statute to align with WIOA in several ways, including performance accountability measures and regional planning methodology.
