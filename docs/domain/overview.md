# Domain: Overview

## The worldview

The domain section documents how the workforce development worldview manifests in the context of California Community Colleges. A worldview is a coherent way of seeing how the world works in a particular domain. The workforce development worldview holds that economic prosperity depends on the alignment between what colleges produce and what the labor market demands, that this alignment is something institutions can work toward deliberately, and that doing so requires shared infrastructure — common vocabularies, authoritative data sources, funding mechanisms, and institutional frameworks that let colleges and industry coordinate at scale.

This worldview is opinionated. It is not the only way to think about what colleges should do or how labor markets should operate. Other framings exist: colleges as primarily for civic education and personal development, labor markets as primarily for individual opportunity rather than institutional alignment, workforce systems as primarily local and informal rather than coordinated and infrastructure-heavy. The workforce development worldview chooses a particular cut. It treats the relationship between curriculum and labor market as a coordinable institutional problem, and it builds out the structures that make coordination possible.

Kallipolis aligns with this worldview. The product is built on the proposition that colleges and industry can be brought into productive relationship through unified data, shared vocabulary, and intelligent synthesis. The domain section makes the worldview Kallipolis aligns with explicit, so a reader can see what the product is participating in.

## How the worldview manifests in California Community Colleges

The worldview takes a particular shape in the California Community College system. California operates the largest community college system in the United States — 116 colleges across 73 districts, organized into regional consortia, governed at the system level by the Chancellor's Office, and supported by an institutional infrastructure that includes funding mechanisms, research arms, planning systems, and shared classification vocabularies.

This is not the only way the workforce development worldview could be enacted. Other states have different governance structures, different funding mechanisms, different research infrastructures, different ways of relating colleges to industry. California's instantiation is particular, and Kallipolis is built specifically for this particular instantiation. The domain section documents the California CC version of the worldview because that is the version Kallipolis is built to participate in.

The four documents that follow cover the components of the California CC instantiation that most directly shape Kallipolis: the funding and compliance structure, the data authorities, the institutional system itself, and the domain vocabulary.

## The four areas

Each area is a different facet of the same coherent worldview as it takes shape in California Community Colleges.

- **[Strong Workforce Program](./strong-workforce-program.md)** — The California state funding and compliance structure through which the worldview is operationalized at scale. It is the mechanism that organizes how community colleges secure the funding required to form workforce partnerships, and its requirements shape what counts as a fundable, defensible partnership project. *(pending)*

- **[Data Authorities](./data-authorities.md)** — The four institutional sources that ground every claim Kallipolis makes about the workforce development reality of California Community Colleges: the Chancellor's Office Management Information System Data Mart for students, college course catalogs for courses, the Centers of Excellence for occupations, and the California Employment Development Department for employers. Each is a named authority responsible for the truth of a particular kind of evidence. *(pending)*

- **[California Community College System](./california-community-college-system.md)** — The institutional structure within which the worldview is enacted. The 116-college system, the 73 districts, the regional consortia, the Chancellor's Office governance, and the system-level coordination that makes it possible to think about workforce development at state scale rather than only at single-college scale. *(pending)*

- **[Glossary](./glossary.md)** — The vocabulary the worldview speaks in. The acronyms, the program codes, the institutional names, and the policy terminology that appear throughout both the domain and product sections. *(pending)*

## How this section relates to Kallipolis

The domain section is in service of the Kallipolis vision. Each document covers its area both as a thing that exists in the world and as a thing that has direct consequences for the product. The Strong Workforce Program is documented because it is the institutional structure that the [strong workforce form](../product/strong-workforce.md) in the product is built to participate in. The data authorities are documented because they are the four foundational sources that the units of analysis in [the ontology](../product/the-ontology.md) trace to. The California Community College system is documented because the [State Atlas](../product/the-atlas.md) and the state-level architectural commitment are built around it. The glossary is documented because the product section uses domain terminology that needs a coherent reference point.

Cross-references between this section and the product section run in both directions. The domain documents reference where each concept is used in the product section. The product documents reference where each domain concept is fully explained here. Together, the two sections describe both what Kallipolis is and the institutional world Kallipolis is built to operate within.
