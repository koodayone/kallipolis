# Data Authorities

Among the components of the workforce development worldview that Kallipolis aligns with, the most consequential for the ontology's epistemic standing is the principle that every claim about institutional reality should trace to a single named authority responsible for that kind of evidence. Kallipolis adopts this principle directly: each of the four units of analysis in the ontology is backed by a single institutional authority, and the four authorities together are what give the ontology its claim to be defensible rather than invented.

## The essence

The data authorities are the four institutional sources that ground every claim Kallipolis makes about California Community Colleges and their workforce development context. They are not selected for technical convenience. They are selected because each one is the recognized institutional source of truth for a specific kind of evidence: students from the Chancellor's Office Management Information System, courses from each college's own catalog, occupations from the Centers of Excellence, employers from the California Employment Development Department. The authority principle is what allows the ontology to stand behind its data without doing original research.

## The four authorities

**Chancellor's Office Management Information System Data Mart (DataMart).** Operated by the California Community Colleges Chancellor's Office, DataMart is the system of record for student-level data across the entire community college system. It provides information about student enrollment, course-taking, student services, outcomes, and demographics, organized for query by administrators, researchers, and stakeholders. It is the authority for student data because it is the institutional system the Chancellor's Office maintains as the authoritative record of who is enrolled in California's community colleges and what they have done.

**College course catalogs.** Each California community college maintains its own course catalog as the institutional declaration of what it teaches. Course catalogs are decentralized in a way the other three authorities are not — there is no single statewide source — but each catalog is authoritative for its own institution. This decentralization is appropriate to the nature of the data: a course is what an institution commits to teach, and the institution itself is the only entity with the authority to make that commitment.

**Centers of Excellence for Labor Market Research (COE).** A network of regional centers operating across California, COE is the leading source of labor market research for the California Community Colleges. It produces sector profiles, career pathway analyses, employment trend data, wage data, and job growth projections, and it serves as the analytical arm that translates state and federal labor market data into intelligence community colleges can plan against. It is the authority for occupation data because its institutional purpose is to produce exactly the kind of labor market analysis that workforce development decisions depend on.

**California Employment Development Department (EDD).** A major state agency whose responsibilities include unemployment insurance, disability insurance, paid family leave, payroll tax administration, and labor market information. EDD's authority for employer data comes from its administrative role: it is the state institution that handles payroll tax for California employers, which means every employer with workers on payroll in the state is in EDD's records. The agency's Labor Market Information Division publishes the analytical product of this administrative reach — workforce statistics, employer counts, industry trends, and occupation-level employment data. EDD is the authority for employer data because its administrative role gives it comprehensive coverage no other source can match.

## Why the principle matters

The four authorities together establish a specific kind of trust: every claim Kallipolis makes about institutional reality can be traced to an institutional source whose job it is to know that kind of thing. When a coordinator looks at a student in Kallipolis, the underlying evidence comes from DataMart. When they look at a course, the underlying evidence comes from that college's own catalog. When they look at an occupation's regional demand, the underlying evidence comes from COE. When they look at an employer's hiring pipeline, the underlying evidence comes from EDD.

This is the partial-by-design principle applied at the level of evidence sourcing. Kallipolis does not blend authorities, does not average across methodologies, and does not invent values. The trade-off is real: the ontology cannot represent claims that no authority is responsible for. But the trade-off is what makes the ontology defensible. A coordinator who needs to defend a claim can trace it to an institutional source whose responsibility is to know that kind of claim, rather than to an analytical pipeline whose intermediate decisions are not visible.

The four authorities are also the boundary of what the ontology can responsibly say. Anything outside their collective coverage is outside the ontology's scope, and that constraint is appropriate to a product built to be trustworthy at scale.

## How this shapes Kallipolis

The data authority principle is what shapes [the ontology](../product/the-ontology.md) at the most fundamental level. The four units of analysis are not chosen for any internal technical reason — they are chosen because each one corresponds to an institutional authority that can stand behind it. The mapping is one-to-one: students to DataMart, courses to college catalogs, occupations to COE, employers to EDD LMID. Anything else in the ontology is either a container (departments, regions) or an emergent layer (skills) that does not have its own authority and is therefore not foundational in the same way.

This is also what gives [the partnership generation flow](../product/partnerships.md) its trust. When Kallipolis assembles the empirical foundation for a partnership opportunity — the regional occupational demand, the curriculum alignment, the student pipeline, the employer match — every piece of evidence in that foundation comes from one of the four authorities. The narrative the AI generates is not asked to stand on its own; it is grounded by being immediately followed by data that traces to a named institutional source. The trust-through-visibility design philosophy works only because the data being made visible has authorities behind it.

The authority principle is not a feature. It is the epistemic foundation that everything else in Kallipolis rests on. Without it, the ontology would be a database with no defensible claims. With it, the ontology is an institutional intelligence layer whose every statement can be traced.
