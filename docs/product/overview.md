# Kallipolis: Overview

## Mission

Kallipolis is a partnership intelligence layer that unifies academic and labor market data for community colleges to forge stronger workforce partnerships with industry.

This is the long arc. Everything the product does today, and everything it will do as it grows, is in service of that sentence. The unit of work is the partnership. The actor is the institution. The contribution is the intelligence the institution brings to bear on the work of building, justifying, and proposing partnerships with industry.

## What the product does today

Today's Kallipolis is built around an ontology that brings academic and labor market data into a single joinable structure. The ontology is organized around two arenas: four *units of analysis* — students, courses, occupations, employers — that ground the representation in institutional authority, and two *units of action* — partnerships and strong workforce — that are formulated from the foundationals to drive the work of partnership formation forward. Each foundational entity traces to a single named institutional source. The ontology does not blend, average, or invent. When a coordinator asks where a number comes from, the answer is one line: it comes from the institution whose job it is to know.

The user encounters the ontology through the *Atlas*, a navigational layer that operates at two scales. At the *College Atlas* scale, the user moves through the six forms of their own institution. At the *State Atlas* scale, they step up one level and navigate the entire California community college system, moving between any college's College Atlas the same way they move through their own. The semantic medium that connects everything is the *unified skills taxonomy* — a deliberately constructed vocabulary that lets courses on the supply side and occupations on the demand side speak the same language about competency.

The core value proposition is the formulation of data-driven partnership opportunities. Partnerships sit at the *discovery stage* of the partnership lifecycle, where a coordinator decides whether a particular relationship with an employer is worth pursuing. Strong workforce sits at the *justification stage*, where the partnership is translated into a fundable project application that complies with the Strong Workforce Program. The two stages share an empirical substrate — the evidence assembled at discovery is the same evidence the justification needs — and together they take a coordinator from initial recognition of an opportunity to a fundable, compliant proposal. Artificial intelligence supports both flows by drafting narrative material grounded in the assembled evidence. The AI is the help, not the headline.

For the full treatment of each component, see [The Ontology](./the-ontology.md), [The Atlas](./the-atlas.md), [The Unified Skills Taxonomy](./the-skills-taxonomy.md), and the dedicated documents for each of the six forms.

## The thesis

The bet Kallipolis is making is that an institution equipped with unified, authoritatively grounded data and AI-augmented intelligence will have the capacity to forge stronger partnerships than an institution working without that foundation.

The product does not claim to make partnerships stronger on its own. It claims to empower the institution with the capacity to do so. The agency remains with the college. The coordinator forges the partnership. The dean approves it. The faculty integrate it. The product's contribution is that the work of building and justifying those partnerships is no longer constrained by the absence of a usable empirical foundation.

This is a paradigm move, not a feature improvement. The traditional way of building college-industry partnerships is constrained by data scattered across disconnected institutional silos and by the time required to assemble defensible labor market justification by hand. Kallipolis introduces a different paradigm: data already joined, evidence assembled in minutes rather than days, and AI that reduces the drafting burden so coordinators can spend their time on judgment and relationships rather than research and writing. Whether this paradigm produces measurably better outcomes is a question that will be answered over time, in partnership with the institutional stakeholders the product serves. The paradigm itself is the current contribution.

## Why this exists

California community colleges are the largest workforce development system in the country. They sit at the intersection of two data worlds that until now have not been brought together in a form that makes the intersection workable. On the academic side are the curricula, students, programs, and outcomes that colleges generate as they teach. On the labor market side are the regional occupations, employers, wages, and skill requirements that describe what the economy is doing. Both sides have institutional authorities responsible for them. Both sides produce real, defensible, recurring data. Neither side has a natural affordance for asking questions of the other.

Kallipolis is the affordance. A coordinator can ask which regional employers hire for the skills their welding program teaches, how strong the alignment is, how many students are in the pipeline for those skills, and what a partnership proposal to one of those employers might look like — and get answers grounded in the actual sources of institutional truth. That question, asked plainly, is what the product is built to make answerable.

## Initial form

The Kallipolis available today is the initial form of a longer arc. The mission persists across versions of the product; the operational capabilities evolve as the ontology grows, the AI matures, and the institutional partnerships that shape what *stronger* actually means develop alongside it. The state-level architecture the State Atlas manifests, the institutional alignment that strong workforce embodies, and the discovery-to-justification lifecycle the two units of action complete are all expressions of where the product is going. What is described above is the product as it exists now, in service of the mission as it has been defined now. Both will sharpen over time.
